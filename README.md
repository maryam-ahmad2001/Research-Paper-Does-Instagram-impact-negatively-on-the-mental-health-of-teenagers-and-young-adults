# Research-Paper-Does-Instagram-impact-negatively-on-the-mental-health-of-teenagers-and-young-adults?

***Overview***

This research paper investigates whether Instagram use has a measurable negative effect on the mental health of teenagers and young adults. The study employed a primary data collection approach via a structured questionnaire, with statistical analysis carried out in IBM SPSS and Microsoft Excel. 

***Motivation***

As Instagram usage surged among younger demographics, concerns about its psychological effects — including anxiety, depression, social comparison, and body image issues — gained increasing attention in both academic and public discourse. The research aimed to test these concerns empirically using real survey data rather than relying solely on anecdotal evidence.

***Methodology***

The research followed a quantitative survey-based design:
1.	A structured questionnaire was designed to capture frequency of Instagram use, self-reported emotional states, and perceived social comparison effects
2.	Responses were collected from a target demographic of teenagers and young adults
3.	Data was cleaned and organised in Microsoft Excel before being imported into IBM SPSS for statistical analysis
4.	Descriptive statistics, frequency distributions, and inferential tests were applied to evaluate the relationship between Instagram usage patterns and mental health indicators
   
***Tools & Technologies***

•	IBM SPSS — statistical hypothesis testing and analysis

•	Microsoft Excel — data organisation and preliminary exploration

•	Questionnaire (primary data collection) — survey design and administration

***Key Findings & Conclusion***

The analysis provided quantitative insights into the degree to which Instagram usage correlated with self-reported negative mental health outcomes. The paper contributed a data-driven perspective to a question that is often discussed qualitatively.
